About Me

I'm Aakarsh Kashyap, an undergraduate student deeply intrested in the space where systems engineering meets machine learning.I care less about abstract models in cloud, and more about what happens when you try to run intelligece directly on a device, whether that's a smartphone, a wearable, or low-power embedded system.

My current focus is building a custom Android OS that runs offline NLP model for direct system control ~no internet, just real-time, low-level interaction. I'm also working on a hearing-aid app using RNNoise and WebAssembly for real-time noise suppression in resource-constrained enviroments

I like understanding things from ground up: I am currently learning Linux internals, system calls, shell scripting, C/C++, and how machine learning models can fit into that stack. My goal isn't just to make things work - it's to make them fast, efficient, and being able to deal with randomness of real world.

I am open to research internship or collaborations where I can push my understanding of embedded ML, OS design, and intelligent system behavior ~ideally someplace I can get my hands dirty, not just write theory
